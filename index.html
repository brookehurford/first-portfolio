My yellow brick road to coding

I can still hear my mom in my head saying, "You should do something with computers". Flash forward 15 years and I had graduated high school, moved to Eugene to pursue Dental Hygiene, and once I figured out that path definitely wasn't me - I landed on full-time server. At that point in my life I loved it, but I knew it wouldn't fulfill me forever.

In the summer of 2011 I decided it was time to move back to Portland, and get a job with my cousin at SightWorks. To be honest - I had little to no idea what they actually did. Turns out they built websites. During the next four years I found my love of this industry. The people, the energy, the learning, the building. As a Project Manager at SightWorks I learned more than I could have ever hoped for. I found that my absolute favorite part of my job was working with my team. Getting in a room, figuring out a problem, and solving it - together. Turns out sometimes you just have to find things yourself, even if it was right in front of you the whole time.
